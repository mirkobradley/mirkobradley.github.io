Bitcoin: The Alternative That Became The Same Thing

On November 19 2025 Nvidia lost 500 billion dollars in market cap in a single day. At the exact same moment Bitcoin crashed from 126,000 to 82,000 dollars. Two assets that were never supposed to have anything in common moved as if they were the very same thing. Because they have become exactly that.

The correlation between Bitcoin and technology stocks reached 0.84 in November 2025. The highest level since 2022. Before 2020 it was close to zero. This means when stock markets fall Bitcoin falls. When Nvidia collapses Bitcoin collapses. The diversification Bitcoin once promised no longer exists.

To understand why this matters we have to go back to what Bitcoin was meant to be.

Bitcoin is a digital currency that does not exist physically and is not issued by any central bank or government. It runs on a network called blockchain: a public and immutable ledger that records every transaction. No one controls it and the total supply is capped at 21 million units. That artificial scarcity became the foundation of its narrative as a store of value. Digital gold for the 21st century.

The original idea was political before it was financial. Bitcoin was born in 2008 just months after the Lehman Brothers collapse. Its anonymous creator Satoshi Nakamoto made the message explicit: an alternative monetary system outside the control of the financial institutions that caused the crisis. A currency for those who no longer trusted banks governments or central bankers. Decentralized by design transparent by architecture immune to manipulation by the traditional system.

For years it worked that way at least in the story. A community of enthusiasts some criminals some visionaries some anarchists. An asset on the margins of traditional finance misunderstood by institutions ignored by big funds.

Then came January 2024. The US SEC approved the first Bitcoin ETFs. Funds listed on the stock exchange that let anyone get exposure to Bitcoin without actually holding it. Who launched them? BlackRock. Fidelity. The very same players Bitcoin was supposed to subvert. In just a few months these funds attracted tens of billions of dollars. Bitcoin entered institutional portfolios pension funds and retail products of major banks. From that moment it stopped being outside the system. It became part of the system managed by the same funds priced by the same algorithms bought and sold by the same investors who buy Nvidia and Microsoft.

The 0.84 correlation is not a technical anomaly. It is the logical consequence of this process. Bitcoin and AI stocks now share the same investor base: those looking for high-risk, high-potential-return assets. When risk appetite drops both are sold at the same time by the same players using the same risk models. They have become the same bet with different names.

The system has a remarkable ability. It absorbs its own alternatives and turns them into itself. Bitcoin wanted to be the answer to 2008. Today it moves in lockstep with Nvidia is custodied by BlackRock and priced by the same algorithms that manage AI stocks. The rebellion has become just another financial product. And the financial product behaves exactly like all the others.

Satoshi Nakamoto left no forwarding address.
