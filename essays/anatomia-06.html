Bitcoin: When the Enemy Buys the Revolution

In January 2024 the US SEC approved the first Bitcoin ETFs. An ETF is a listed financial product that lets anyone gain exposure to Bitcoin without ever holding it. No wallet, no private keys, no hassle. You just buy shares like you would buy Fiat stock.

Who launched these ETFs? BlackRock. Fidelity. JPMorgan. The very same institutions Bitcoin was created to subvert.

Today BlackRock holds 805,000 Bitcoin through its ETF, with 75 billion dollars in assets under management. Fidelity exceeds 20 billion. 96 percent of institutional investors say they believe in the long-term value of digital assets. The instrument of rebellion has become Wall Street’s favorite product.

But the final twist did not come from Wall Street. It came from the White House.

In March 2025 Donald Trump signed an executive order to create a national strategic Bitcoin reserve. Same concept as the gold reserve at Fort Knox, except digital. The American state is now accumulating Bitcoin as a strategic asset. Not to free citizens from government control, but to project American power in the global financial system. Trump declared he wants the United States to become the “Bitcoin Superpower” of the world.

To grasp the scale of this reversal, remember what Trump said about Bitcoin in 2019. He tweeted: “I am not a fan of Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies. They are not money and are based on nothing.” Bitcoin dropped 7 percent in 45 minutes. Six years later, the same man signs the order for a national strategic reserve.

Meanwhile, Trump and Melania launched their own memecoins, $TRUMP and $MELANIA. A memecoin is a cryptocurrency with no underlying technology, whose price depends entirely on hype. The promoters called them “an expression of support” rather than an investment. The problem is that the President of the United States, who signs the laws regulating crypto, now has a direct financial interest in the value of a crypto asset. A blatant, declared conflict of interest. No consequences.

Eric Trump and Donald Trump Jr. spoke at the Bitcoin 2025 Conference in Las Vegas, predicting Bitcoin would reach 150,000-175,000 dollars by the end of 2026, while openly highlighting their own involvement in the sector. The family running the most powerful country on earth makes public price predictions on an asset in which they are personally invested.

This is the point: Bitcoin was born as a peer-to-peer system without intermediaries, without banks, without central control. A technology designed to make the state irrelevant in financial transactions. Sixteen years later, its largest institutional holder is BlackRock, its biggest political sponsor is Trump, and the US government is accumulating it as a tool of geopolitical power.

The rebellion was not defeated. It was simply bought at market price, management fees included.

Satoshi Nakamoto imagined a system beyond the control of the powerful. The powerful opened an account.

The price of the revolution is now listed on the stock exchange. Ticker: IBIT.
