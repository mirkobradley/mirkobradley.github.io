SRT: How to Package a Disaster

May 2026. JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs and Citi are desperately trying to dump billions in AI data center debt. The instrument is called SRT — Significant Risk Transfer. Beautiful name. Pure financial poetry. Instead of killing the risk, they just move it somewhere else: to pension funds, private credit shops, hedge funds — anyone dumb enough to buy a position they didn’t build and don’t understand.

In 2025 AI accounted for over a third of all global private credit deals, up from 17% five years earlier. The banks financed the great data center gold rush with hard-ons and smiles. Now they’ve hit their internal exposure limits and need bagholders. Some loans are already being offered at a discount. When investment bankers say the numbers are “beyond anything we’ve ever seen,” that’s polite speak for: we have no fucking clue how to get out of this.

Anyone who lived through 2008 recognizes the pattern. Banks build the monster, collect the fees, then when it gets too big to hide they repackage it and call it “prudent risk management.”

The Financial Stability Board has already flagged it: the concentration of foundational AI models in three or four players is repeating the exact same geometry as pre-2008. If one collapses, the contagion is systemic. Back then it was liquidity risk. Today it’s cognitive access risk.

I wrote it in the paper “The Trap”: when every relevant agent uses the same models to make decisions, the behavioral diversity that historically absorbed systemic shocks disappears. The system collapses in sync, amplified by the very tools that made it grow.

And the best part? The banks now selling SRTs on AI data centers are using AI models to assess the risk of those same SRTs. The loop is closed. Beautifully, fatally closed.

The risk didn’t disappear. It just changed address. As always.

Sources: Financial Times, 3 May 2026 · Financial Stability Board, October 2025 · FSB, November 2024
