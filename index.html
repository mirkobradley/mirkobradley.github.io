About

I have worked with complex systems since childhood. At four, my mother found me lying on the floor, studying an ant colony. Having left the ants to their business, I turned to complex systems at the human-machine intersection, where the mechanisms of power conceal themselves behind an apparently innocent face.

After my doctorate, completed between Germany and the United Kingdom, I spent several years in academic research, then moved into education and training, alongside thirty years of uninterrupted private consultancy in cybersecurity.
