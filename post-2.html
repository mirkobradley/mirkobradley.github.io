How I Think: From Configuration to Formal Rigor and Back

Before entering the theory, it is useful to say something about method, simply to clarify the kind of path you will find in this and other series. My way of thinking and working unfolds through three recursive movements that follow no fixed order, yet call each other, overlap, and transform one another.

The first impulse is always the overall structure. It is an old gesture. As a child I spent hours watching anthills and saw a system of trajectories, points of fragility, imperfect symmetries, and sudden deviations whenever an obstacle altered the path. Those micro-variations, which I now associate with the stochastic, revealed a form reorganising itself in real time. That way of looking has remained unchanged. Today the big picture often emerges in the background while I am doing something else. Connections surface when the mind has already processed what attention has not yet reached. It is a form of distributed elaboration over time. This vision is a configuration made of distant patterns that converge and of structures that repeat across domains that appear unrelated. For example, mind, history, AI, and the collapse of empires follow similar geometries and share the same dynamics of breakdown. These are not decorative analogies, but conceptual and mathematical links of considerable depth.

When the general configuration is clear, I choose a node and open it. Here mathematics enters as a tool of clarification. Formalisation brings to light ambiguities that previously remained invisible. It is the most demanding moment of the work, because it requires precise choices, sharp definitions, and a language that offers no escape routes.

After the detail, I return to the structure. Each opened node modifies the overall form. Some connections strengthen, others shift. The theory recalibrates itself at every iteration. The path does not advance in a straight line; it is recursive and twists as it progresses, a kind of spiral that moves gradually toward the centre.

My sensitivity to complex and distant patterns is pre-verbal. I sense the coherence or incoherence of a structure before I can explain it. It is an internal sensor that evaluates the stability of forms. This allows me to perceive connections that a more linear approach would struggle to detect. At the same time, it requires constant translation work. I transform intuition into concept, concept into form, and form into something that can be shared. This is where the tension that runs through everything I write originates, where a wide vision and formal precision pursue and correct each other.

The path we will follow reflects this method. First the configuration, then the concepts, then the form, and from the form back to the concepts and the configuration. The mathematics, presented here in an essential way, will appear when the ground is ready, as the natural consequence of a line of reasoning already matured. The aim is to build a language that makes visible what the mind does before words.
